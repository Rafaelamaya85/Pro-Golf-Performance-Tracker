# REFLECTION.md (Shortened)

Building *The Heat Index* was alot of fun and incredibly rewarding because it connects to something I genuinely care about. As a golf obsessed fan, I wanted to create a simple way to identify the best golfers right now. Golf in general is a complex sport that can be overwhelming. The rankings are often difficult to understand because they rely on complex systems that include results from the past two to three years. Unlike other sports that focus on current performance, golf can make it hard to see who is actually in form and not benefitting from a hot streak from a few years ago. This project was my attempt to simplify that.

One of my main decisions was to keep the formula straightforward by focusing on wins, top 10 finishes, majors, and missed cuts. This made the rankings easier to understand and more accessible. I also designed the layout to be clean and easy to scan, using elements like leaderboard cards, a featured player section, and stat summaries to guide the user.

The site evolved a lot from beginning to end. My initial Figma design was clear, but once I started building it, I ran into layout and usability issues. The code just did not transfer as I expected so I had to adjust. Testing with peers and friends helped me identify problem areas and improve the site over time. Small adjustments to spacing, organization, and visual hierarchy made a big difference, and the final version feels much more polished.

If I had more time, I would look to make the data dynamic instead of manually updating it as the more the site grows the more time consuming it can be to update it each individual section and player. I would look to improve the mobile experience further, and add features like search or player comparisons as well. I also learned how important iteration and design decisions are. Small changes to the different iterations helped me evolve the page in a manageable way that greatly improved usability and overall experience.

Overall, this project helped me turn a personal pet peeve about golf into an exciting functional product. It also allowed me to combine my interest in sports, my IT expereince, web development skills from the CMPA program and create something even my 11 year old can understand.
